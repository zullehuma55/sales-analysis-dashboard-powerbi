# sales-analysis-dashboard-powerbi
Interactive Sales Analysis Dashboard built using Power BI to analyze sales trends, profitability, and regional performance.
📊 Sales Analysis Dashboard – Power BI
📌 Project Overview
This project presents an interactive Sales Analysis Dashboard built using Power BI to analyze business performance across products, regions, and customer segments.
The dashboard provides insights into sales trends, profitability, product performance, and regional contributions, enabling stakeholders to quickly evaluate business performance and identify growth opportunities.

🎯 Business Objectives
The dashboard aims to answer key business questions:
How are sales and profit trending over time?
Which product categories drive the highest revenue?
What are the top-performing products?
Which regions contribute the most sales?
How has profit margin changed over time?

📊 Dashboard Features
KPI Overview
Total Sales
Total Profit
Total Orders
Profit Margin %
Each KPI includes:
Month-over-Month (MoM) performance
Comparison with previous year
Sales Trend Analysis
Monthly sales trend across multiple years
YoY comparison using line charts
Tooltip insights for deeper analysis
Category Performance
Bar chart showing sales by product category to identify the most profitable business segments.
Regional Performance
Regional comparison of sales across:
West
East
Central
South

Product Insights
Top 5 products ranked by total sales to highlight key revenue drivers.

Profitability Analysis
Scatter plot visualizing Sales vs Profit by product, helping identify:

High revenue but low profit products

High margin opportunities

🛠 Tools & Technologies
Power BI – Data visualization & dashboard creation
DAX – Calculated measures (MoM, YoY, Profit Margin)
Power Query – Data transformation
Data Modeling – Star schema with date table

📈 Key Insights
Technology category generates the highest sales revenue.
The West region leads overall sales performance.
Sales show consistent growth across recent years.
Some products generate high sales but relatively lower profit margins, indicating potential pricing or cost optimization opportunities.
